# Grid Interconnection Copilot Marketing Plan

## 1. Market context

Grid Interconnection Copilot is entering a market with obvious pain and growing urgency:

- The U.S. interconnection backlog reached nearly 2,600 GW of active generation and storage capacity by the end of 2023, according to Berkeley Lab's 2024 "Queued Up" edition.
- The U.S. Department of Energy has highlighted both long queue delays and steeply rising upgrade costs, with PJM and MISO seeing major cost inflation for active applications.
- The International Energy Agency's February 25, 2025 transmission report underscores that transmission expansion and supply-chain constraints are now strategic bottlenecks for clean-energy deployment.
- Commercial tools such as Yes Energy GridSite validate willingness to pay for decision-grade siting and interconnection intelligence, but the market is still fragmented across queue data, mapping, diligence workflows, and investment memo generation.

This gives the product a clear wedge: help renewable developers and infrastructure investors decide where to spend diligence dollars before they commit engineering, legal, and land-acquisition budgets.

## 2. Product positioning

### Positioning statement

Grid Interconnection Copilot is a renewable-development workflow product that helps teams screen candidate sites faster, document why one site outranks another, and turn raw site attributes into repeatable diligence decisions.

### Category

- Primary category: interconnection and siting decision workspace
- Secondary category: development diligence workflow software

### What makes the product different

- It combines screening, ranking, exportable memos, and team workflow in one workspace.
- It is designed for fast "should we spend the next dollar here?" decisions, not just data lookup.
- It gives analysts and deal teams structured outputs they can share with leadership.
- It is lighter-weight and faster to operationalize than a full data-stack replacement.

## 3. Ideal customer profile

### Primary buyers

- Director of development at utility-scale solar and storage developers
- Vice president of origination or siting at independent power producers
- Infrastructure investment teams screening renewable pipelines
- Energy transition private equity and strategic development teams

### Primary users

- Development analysts
- GIS and siting associates
- Interconnection managers
- Investment associates
- Project finance teams preparing screening materials

### Best first segment

The best starting segment is U.S. solar-and-storage developers with 10-200 active pipeline opportunities across PJM, MISO, SPP, ERCOT, and CAISO. They have repeated screening needs, spreadsheet fatigue, and enough workflow complexity to value standardized ranking and exports.

## 4. Core customer problems

The product should market against these specific pains:

1. Teams waste weeks on early-stage opportunities that should have been screened out in hours.
2. Interconnection, permitting, and community risk signals live across fragmented spreadsheets and analyst notes.
3. Leadership reviews are slowed down by inconsistent diligence formats.
4. New analysts take too long to become productive because the screening logic is tribal knowledge.
5. Portfolio managers want a comparable ranking view across candidate sites, not isolated project notes.

## 5. Messaging framework

### Top-level message

Screen renewable sites faster, document the rationale, and focus diligence on the opportunities most likely to survive interconnection and siting friction.

### Message pillars

#### A. Save diligence time

- Move from ad hoc screening to repeatable first-pass scoring.
- Reduce wasted engineering, legal, and landowner outreach on weak sites.

#### B. Make screening defendable

- Standardize why a site is strong, watchlist, or high-risk.
- Give investment and development teams a shared language for tradeoffs.

#### C. Shorten decision cycles

- Export portfolio views and readiness memos without rebuilding the analysis in PowerPoint.
- Help internal champions bring better materials into pipeline and investment committee discussions.

#### D. Improve team consistency

- Make the screening framework visible, reusable, and auditable.
- Reduce dependence on one experienced analyst's mental model.

### Example taglines

- "Spend diligence where it matters."
- "From raw sites to ranked pipeline."
- "A faster first-pass for interconnection and siting risk."

## 6. Competitive landscape

### Direct and adjacent competitors

- Yes Energy GridSite
- Pivvot
- Kevala
- Internal GIS plus spreadsheet workflows
- Consultant-led interconnection and siting screening

### Competitive angle

Grid Interconnection Copilot should not try to win on "we have all the power-market data." Larger vendors already have deeper data distribution. It should win on:

- workflow speed
- structured analyst output
- ease of adoption
- consistent ranking
- memo and export readiness
- fast deployment for small and midsize development teams

### Positioning against incumbents

- Against enterprise data vendors: simpler, faster, lower-friction workflow adoption
- Against spreadsheets: more consistency, better auditability, easier sharing
- Against consultants: reusable internal process instead of one-off external studies

## 7. Pricing and packaging

### Recommended pilot packaging

- 6- to 8-week pilot
- fixed fee with onboarding and white-glove setup
- includes admin access, up to a defined number of users, and success review

### Recommended post-pilot packaging

- Annual platform fee for workspace access
- Seat-based pricing for analysts and managers
- Premium modules later for external data connectors, API access, and advanced reporting

### Pricing strategy

Do not lead with low-cost self-serve pricing. Start with consultative pricing because the buyer is comparing the product against analyst time, consultant spend, and missed pipeline opportunities, not generic SaaS benchmarks.

## 8. Go-to-market strategy

### Phase 1: founder-led outbound

Target:

- developers in PJM, MISO, and ERCOT
- storage-heavy and solar-plus-storage teams
- infrastructure investors screening multiple small-to-mid pipeline opportunities

Outbound motions:

- tailored cold outreach to heads of development and siting
- LinkedIn outreach using recent project announcements and queue activity as context
- pilot offers framed around a small live pipeline review

### Phase 2: content-led demand generation

Content themes:

- interconnection queue bottlenecks by market
- screening mistakes that waste diligence budget
- what a "good" first-pass siting memo looks like
- how analysts can standardize portfolio triage

Content formats:

- short market briefings
- downloadable screening templates
- before-and-after case studies
- webinars with a siting or interconnection expert

### Phase 3: partnerships

Potential partners:

- boutique renewable diligence consultancies
- energy law firms with project-development practices
- interconnection engineering advisors
- industry communities and newsletters covering renewable development

## 9. Demand generation channels

### Highest-priority channels

1. Founder-led outbound email and LinkedIn
2. Referral introductions from renewable advisors
3. Webinars and reports tied to queue bottlenecks
4. Targeted conference meetings at renewable-development events
5. Search-optimized content around interconnection delays, siting risk, and renewable pipeline screening

### Lower-priority channels for now

- broad paid social
- generic startup PR
- freemium self-serve acquisition

## 10. Sales motion

### Sales process

1. Discovery call on current siting and screening workflow
2. Pilot scoping on a small portfolio of active sites
3. Live analyst walkthrough using the buyer's real examples
4. Success review with time-saved and workflow-quality evidence
5. Annual conversion with team rollout

### Pilot success metrics

- number of candidate sites screened
- time from intake to ranked output
- number of leadership-ready memos generated
- user activation across analysts and managers
- qualitative confidence improvement in screening calls

## 11. Customer proof strategy

### Early proof assets to build

- one anonymized case study showing faster pipeline triage
- one sample readiness memo
- one benchmark worksheet comparing old spreadsheet workflow vs product workflow
- one quote from a development or investment user

### Social proof plan

The first customer proof should emphasize workflow acceleration and better decision quality, not dramatic claims about guaranteed project success. Credible operational proof will sell better than inflated ROI language.

## 12. 90-day launch plan

### Days 1-30

- tighten the website and landing copy around siting-screening workflow
- publish a flagship article on interconnection and siting triage
- create demo dataset plus a sample export package
- identify 50 named target accounts
- start founder-led outbound

### Days 31-60

- run 10-15 discovery calls
- close 2-3 structured pilots
- publish one customer-style workflow case study
- host one webinar on early-stage site screening

### Days 61-90

- convert the strongest pilot into an annual contract
- refine pricing and packaging from pilot objections
- add new content based on the most repeated buyer questions
- turn pilot results into sales collateral

## 14. Risks and countermeasures

### Risk: buyers see the product as a "nice-to-have dashboard"

Countermeasure:

- demo against real workflow pain
- lead with time saved and decision consistency
- show exports and memos, not just UI screens

### Risk: larger vendors out-market the product

Countermeasure:

- focus on narrower workflow ownership
- position as easier to adopt and more actionable
- move quickly on customer-informed features

### Risk: limited trust without external data depth

Countermeasure:

- be explicit about screening scope
- add connector roadmap in sales conversations
- publish transparent methodology and output examples
